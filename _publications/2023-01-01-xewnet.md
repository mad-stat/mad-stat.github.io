Forecasting dengue outbreaks is critical for public health planning, yet existing models often struggle with nonlinear dynamics and external climatic influences. This work proposes *XEWNet*, an ensemble wavelet neural network that incorporates exogenous climate variables to improve forecasting accuracy and reliability. The model captures complex relationships while remaining interpretable and computationally efficient.
